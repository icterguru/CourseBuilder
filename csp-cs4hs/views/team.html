CS Principles for High School Team

Instructor

Jeff Gray

Dr. Jeff Gray teaches Computer Science at the University of Alabama (UA). He received a PhD from Vanderbilt University, and a BS/MS from West Virginia University, all in Computer Science. A recipient of the National Science Foundation (NSF) CAREER award, Jeff also was named the 2008 Alabama Professor of the Year by the Carnegie Foundation. Jeff has been a CS Principles Pilot instructor since 2011 and is the Principal Investigator of the CS4Alabama project, which is funded by the NSF. His K-12 outreach includes organizing summer camps and robotics contests, while also mentoring high school students on science fair projects (four INTEL ISEF finalists). At UA, he mentors undergraduate and PhD students on topics related to Software Engineering. Jeff also serves on the Education Advisory Council of Code.org.

Other additional information about Jeff is available here .

Guest Speakers

Michele Roberts

Michele Roberts teaches in the Computer Science department on the Indianapolis campus of Purdue University. Her teaching focus includes Data Science, Software Engineering and Computer Science Fundamentals. She is active in many computing outreach programs, working along side K-12 colleagues to help produce a new generation of computational thinkers.

Michele will provide a detailed lesson and associated activities associated with Data.

Owen Astrachan

Dr. Owen Astrachan is the Director of Undergraduate Studies and Professor of the Practice of Computer Science at Duke University. He is a recipient of the NSF CAREER award and is an NSF-CISE Education Fellow. Owen is also a Distinguished Member of the ACM.

As the PI of the CS Principles project, Owen Astrachan works to help the community of educators working on this and related projects achieve their goals.

Owen will share his thoughts about the opportunities and impact that is possible with CS Principles.

Brook Osborne

Brook Osborne is the 9-12 Program Manager at Code.org. Previously, she was the Outreach Director and pilot coordinator for CS Principles and the National Director of Outreach in Computer Science at Duke University.

Andy Kuemmel

Andy Kuemmel has taught high school computer science and mathematics for 25 years and earned certification from the National Board for Professional Teaching Standards in 1998. He taught CS Principles as a College Board pilot instructor at Madison West High School and at the University of Wisconsin-Madison. He is the founding president of the CSTA-Dairyland Wisconsin chapter and is working with the Wisconsin Department of Public Instruction to revise their Computer Science Teacher Licensing Requirements. Don't tell his wife and kids, but Andy secretly enjoys cleaning his house.
